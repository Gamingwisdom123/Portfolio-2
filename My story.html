My Life Story — By Wisdom

Written with honesty and heart, by an 11-year-old who loves football and gaming.

My name is Wisdom. I was born in the lively country of Nigeria and moved to the United Kingdom when I was just eight years old. Moving was not easy, but it taught me how to adapt and stay brave in new places. I still remember the first day I saw snow — it felt like magic falling from the sky!

Back in Nigeria, I used to play football almost every day with my friends. We didn’t always have a proper ball, but that never stopped us. We made one from plastic bags and string, and it worked perfectly. Those days taught me that joy doesn’t come from what you have, but from what you make of it.

My biggest football hero is Cristiano Ronaldo. I used to spell his name as rnaldo when I was younger, but now I know it by heart because he inspires me every single day. What I love about him is his hard work, confidence, and never-give-up attitude. I try to play like him — always focused, always pushing myself.

When I first arrived in the UK, I was shy and missed my old friends. But football helped me make new ones quickly. It’s amazing how one game can connect people no matter where they’re from. Every time I score a goal, I feel that same excitement I had playing on the streets of Lagos.

I also enjoy gaming in my free time. Some of my favourite games are the ones that let me build, explore, or compete — they make me think fast and plan smart. My dream is to one day create my own football game, where players can learn teamwork while having fun.

At school, my favourite subjects are Math and ICT. I love working with computers and discovering how they work. Coding feels like solving a big, exciting puzzle. I believe that if I keep learning, one day I’ll use my knowledge to build something amazing — maybe a game that inspires other kids too.

My parents always tell me that success doesn’t happen overnight. They say, “Be patient and keep practicing.” That’s why I always remind myself that every mistake is just another step toward improvement. Even when things go wrong, I don’t give up — I just try again.

Sometimes I think about how far I’ve come — from playing football barefoot under the hot sun in Nigeria, to training in the cool air of the UK. Life has changed a lot, but one thing hasn’t: my love for the game and my dream to be great.

Every morning, I wake up and remind myself that hard work and discipline always pay off. Whether I’m doing homework, practicing football, or gaming, I always try to give my best because I want to make my family proud — and prove to myself that I can do anything I set my mind to.

When I imagine the future, I see myself standing on a big football pitch, wearing a jersey with my name on the back, hearing the crowd cheer as I score a goal. But I also see myself helping others, teaching kids to believe in themselves the way I do. That’s what I want my story to be about — dreams, effort, and never giving up.

Written with passion by Wisdom, age 11 — proud Nigerian, football lover, and future champion.
